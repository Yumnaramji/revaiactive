Featured story

★★★★★
"I started running at 45. I never thought that sentence would belong to me."

Laila Christensen is a nurse practitioner in Nairobi. She has worked 12-hour shifts for most of her adult life, raised two children mostly alone, and spent years telling herself that exercise was something other people did — people with more time, more energy, or a different kind of body. Earlier this year she found herself, for the first time in nearly two decades, with evenings that belonged to her.

"A colleague suggested I try a Couch to 5K programme. I almost laughed at her. I told her I didn't own running shoes, let alone running clothes. She said that was fine — start with what you have. I did. I went out in an old pair of cotton leggings and a T-shirt. By week three I was sweating through everything by kilometre one and chafing so badly on my inner thighs that I nearly quit." Laila found REVAÍ shortly after the brand launched. She ordered a pair on a Tuesday. By Thursday she was running again.

"It sounds small, I know. But wearing something that actually fits — that moves with you instead of against you — it changes what running feels like. I finished my first 5K race last month. It took me 42 minutes and I cried at the finish line. I'm doing my second race next month. I'm still not fast. I will probably never be fast. But I go out three mornings a week now and I genuinely look forward to it, which is something I never thought I'd say about anything before 7am."

LC

Laila Christensen

Nurse Practitioner · Nairobi, Kenya · Running
