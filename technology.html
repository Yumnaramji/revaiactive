01 — Fabric System

Four-Way
Stretch

Most activewear stretches in two directions. Ours moves in four — horizontally, vertically, and diagonally — because your body doesn't follow a grid. Whether you're dropping into a squat, reaching overhead, or twisting through a sprint, the fabric moves with you rather than against you.

We achieve this through a proprietary weave structure that interlinks elastane fibers at multiple axis points rather than embedding them in parallel. The result is stretch that feels unrestricted in every direction while snapping back to its original shape thousands of washes later.

The elasticity rating is tested to 150% extension with less than 3% permanent deformation after 10,000 stretch cycles — meaning your gear still fits like day one after years of hard use.

150% stretch capacity <3% permanent deformation 10,000 cycle tested

02 — Temperature Management

Thermal
Regulation

Your body generates heat unevenly. Your activewear should respond to that. REVAÍ garments use a zone-mapped construction that places moisture-wicking, open-weave panels exactly where your body runs hottest — underarms, upper back, inner thighs — while keeping denser, wind-resistant fabric over your core.

The outer face of the fabric is treated with a hydrophobic finish that pushes moisture away from the skin surface, while the inner face is brushed to maintain a slight thermal layer in cooler conditions. The result is a garment that adapts rather than just evacuates heat.

Lab-tested across a temperature range of -5°C to 35°C, thermal regulation reduces perceived exertion temperature by an average of 1.8°C compared to unzoned activewear — a small number that makes a meaningful difference in performance and comfort.

-5°C to 35°C range Zone-mapped construction Hydrophobic outer face Moisture-wicking inner

03 — Freshness System

Silver-Ion
Odor Control

Odor doesn't come from sweat — it comes from bacteria breaking down sweat on fabric surfaces. By embedding silver ions directly into the fiber at the yarn stage (not as a surface coating that washes away), we interrupt that process at the source. Silver ions are naturally antimicrobial and have been used in medical textiles for decades.

Unlike topical antimicrobial treatments that fade after 20–30 washes, our fiber-integrated silver ions remain active for the life of the garment. We use only the minimum effective concentration — 0.1% by weight — so you get the benefit without unnecessary chemical load on the environment.

Third-party tested to AATCC 100 antimicrobial standards, our silver-ion treated fabrics show greater than 99.9% reduction in bacterial growth after 100 industrial wash cycles. Your gear stays fresher, longer — which also means you wash it less, extending its life further.

Fiber-integrated (not surface coated) 99.9% bacterial reduction AATCC 100 certified 0.1% silver by weight

04 — Compression Engineering

Graduated
Compression

Not all compression is useful compression. Uniform tightness can restrict circulation and increase fatigue. Graduated compression applies targeted pressure that's highest at the extremities and reduces progressively toward the core — working with your circulatory system to push blood back toward the heart, reduce muscle vibration, and speed recovery.

Our compression tights and sleeves are constructed with variable yarn density zones — tighter weaves at the ankle and calf, opening up progressively toward the hip. This is achieved without seams or panels, maintaining a smooth silhouette while delivering medically informed pressure gradients.

Compression levels range from 15–20 mmHg (light, everyday support) in our Core tier to 20–30 mmHg (performance support) in our Pro line — ranges clinically associated with improved endurance and reduced delayed onset muscle soreness (DOMS).

15–20 mmHg (Core) 20–30 mmHg (Pro) Variable yarn density No-seam zone mapping

05 — Construction

Flatlock Seam
Construction

Standard seam construction stacks fabric layers on top of each other, creating a ridge that chafes under repetitive movement. Flatlock seaming lays the seam flat against the skin — joining panels edge-to-edge so the transition between them is almost imperceptible. Over a two-hour run, that difference matters enormously.

We use a 5-thread flatlock on all high-contact zones — inner thigh, underarm, shoulder — and a reinforced 8-thread flatlock on waistbands and hems that bear repeated tension. The thread itself is a bonded polyester with 30% more tensile strength than standard activewear thread, meaning seams stay flat and intact under years of washing and wear.

For select Pro tier garments, we further eliminate seams in the highest-friction zones entirely, using bonded tape construction — a thin, flexible adhesive laminate that joins panels without thread. The result is a surface with zero raised profile against skin.

5-thread flatlock (contact zones) 8-thread flatlock (hems/waist) Bonded tape (Pro tier) 30% stronger thread

06 — Sun Protection

UV
Protection

Training outdoors exposes your skin to cumulative UV damage that standard activewear does nothing to prevent. REVAÍ integrates UPF 50+ sun protection directly into the fabric construction — not as a chemical wash that degrades, but through tight weave geometry and UV-absorbing fiber blends that physically block ultraviolet radiation.

UPF 50+ blocks more than 98% of both UVA and UVB rays — the equivalent of wearing factor 50 sunscreen across your covered skin. This matters especially for high-output outdoor activities like running, padel, and golf, where re-application of sunscreen mid-session is impractical and sweating accelerates SPF breakdown.

UV protection is built into all REVAÍ pieces intended for outdoor use and remains effective for the life of the garment — tested to retain greater than 97% of its UPF rating after 60 wash cycles. The fabric feels no different. The protection is structural, not chemical.

UPF 50+ rated Blocks 98%+ UVA & UVB Structural (not chemical) 97%+ retained after 60 washes
